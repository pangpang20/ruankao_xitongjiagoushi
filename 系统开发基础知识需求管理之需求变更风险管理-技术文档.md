### 1.1 Concept of Requirements Change | éœ€æ±‚å˜æ›´çš„æ¦‚å¿µ

**English:**

**Requirements Change** refers to any modification, addition, or deletion to the agreed-upon requirements baseline during the software development lifecycle.

**Definition Components**:
- **Baseline**: A formally approved version of requirements serving as a reference point
- **Modification**: Alteration of existing requirements specifications
- **Addition**: Introduction of new requirements not in the original scope
- **Deletion**: Removal of previously agreed requirements

**Key Characteristics | å…³é”®ç‰¹å¾**:

1. **Inevitability | ä¸å¯é¿å…æ€§**
   - Change is a natural part of software development
   - Occurs regardless of methodology (Waterfall, Agile, DevOps)
   - Average project experiences 25-35% requirements change rate

2. **Impact | å½±å“æ€§**
   - Affects project scope, schedule, cost, quality, and resources
   - Ripple effects across architecture, design, code, testing, documentation
   - May introduce new risks or exacerbate existing ones

3. **Traceability | å¯è¿½æº¯æ€§**
   - Changes must be linked to business justification
   - Forward traceability to design, code, tests
   - Backward traceability to original requirements

4. **Risk | é£é™©æ€§**
   - Each change introduces potential for defects
   - Late-stage changes are exponentially more expensive
   - Unmanaged changes lead to scope creep and project failure
